3 points that film lovers need to pay attention to

A film's storyline and how it is presented to the audience are critical factors. Critics should analyze the coherence of the plot, character development, and the overall narrative structure. They should assess how well the story engages the audience, whether it effectively conveys the intended message, and if it successfully elicits emotional or intellectual responses from viewers.

Film critics should evaluate the technical aspects of a movie, including cinematography, sound design, editing, and visual effects. They should examine how these elements contribute to the overall atmosphere, mood, and thematic exploration of the film. Assessing the use of lighting, camera angles, and shot composition can provide insights into the director's vision and execution.

The performances of the actors and their ability to bring the characters to life are pivotal aspects in any film. Critics should analyze the actors' interpretations, their emotional range, and how convincingly they convey the intended roles. They should pay attention to the chemistry between the characters and assess whether the performances contribute to the authenticity and depth of the narrative.
